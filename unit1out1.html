1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT in modern business is needed to communicate fast and efficiently with individuals, organisations or IT systems. Modern businesses also rely on IT to process data and keep up with market intelligence. IT in modern business plays an important role in all types of industry, which helps improve processes within a business, help to competitively maintain an advantage in the marketplace and achieve cost efficiencies.

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

Depending on the project and whether or not you are working individually or collaboratively within a team, there are methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully. Methods I would use are documenting all research and sharing it within my team if it is a collaboration. I would also implement regular reviews (stand-ups) to monitor progress. Tools which could be useful are but not limited to; Trello, Slack and Google Drive. Trello can be used as a digital version of a kanban board to monitor progress of tasks within a project (what needs to be done, what is being worked on and what has been completed). Slack is a great tool for communicating with a team working on a project from anywhere in the world. Features of Slack; video calling, sharing code, text, images to keep up to date with all aspects of a project. Google Drive can be used to create, edit, store and share different types of documents with individuals which helps to keep content fresh. Skills needed would have to be a good knowledge of aspects of a project with a good ability to problem solve.

1.3 Plan and carry out tasks using IT – e.g. making your website. How will you plan it? Include a link to your Trello board.

I am going to plan out making my website by breaking down each task and placing them onto my Trello board. I can then use the kanban method so I can see what needs to be done, what I am working on and what I have completed. When I am working on a specific task I will move it into the ‘WIP’ section of my Trello board, then once completed move it into the ‘Done’ section of my Trello board. This process will loop and continue until I have finished all the tasks I have assigned myself to be completed.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

The risks that might impact digital projects are not having the right tools, skills, experience or access to expertise/information to successfully produce projects to a high standard. The reasons potentially why digital projects fail are;

1) Lack of knowledge, experience or expertise for team members working on a project.

2) Lack of access to the right tools, for example;
hardware or software based tools that are needed to achieve a desired goal.

3) Lack of planning or research on a project which would help with getting things right first time. Having to redo part or all of a project could potentially have financial implications.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The GDPR replaces the data protection directive [1995] (UK law was based upon this directive) and is a new framework for data protection laws within Europe. GDPR was created to modernise the laws that are in place to protect personal information of individuals and came into force on 25th May 2018. This replaces data protection laws across Europe which were created during the 1990’s and gives individuals more rights/control regarding their personal information. Also it alters how information regarding customers of businesses and public sector organisations is handled/stored. GDPR enforces data minimisation for all businesses/organisations that use to store/collect data for potential future use. So the impact on digital projects is to redesign to take into account the GDPR regulations and not complying to GDPR could have negative financial implications for the individuals/organisations involved.
